1. Knowledge and understanding of engineering.

Maintain and extend knowledge of engineering theory and practice, and how technilogy assists its application.
  • Continuing Education: Mention any relevant engineering courses, workshops, or certifications you have completed. Provide details about the subjects covered and how they have contributed to your knowledge.
  • Engineering Publications: If you have authored or co-authored engineering publications, technical papers, or articles, include them on your portfolio. Explain the significance and impact of your work.
  • Professional Memberships: Mention any memberships in engineering organizations or institutions. Participation in such organizations often requires continuous learning and engagement in the field.
  • Engineering Projects: Describe engineering projects you've worked on, highlighting the theoretical knowledge and practical skills you applied. Discuss how your understanding of engineering theory was put into practice in these projects.
  • Technical Presentations: If you have given presentations on engineering topics at conferences or seminars, mention these and provide links or materials related to your presentations.
  • Personal Projects: If you've undertaken personal engineering projects or experiments, discuss them, detailing how you applied engineering theory and technology.
Solve engineering problems using sound theoretical approach, based on evidence, and contribute to continuous improvement.
  • Engineering Problem-Solving: Provide specific examples of engineering problems you've encountered and how you approached them with a strong theoretical foundation. Discuss the solutions you implemented.
  • Case Studies: Present case studies of challenging engineering projects you've been involved in. Explain the problem, your approach, and the outcomes, focusing on the use of evidence and theory.
  • Quality Improvement Initiatives: Describe any instances where you've been part of efforts to enhance engineering processes or methodologies. Discuss the results and how they contributed to continuous improvement.
  • Evidence of Impact: Provide data or evidence that demonstrates the positive impact of your engineering solutions. This could be in terms of cost savings, increased efficiency, or improved safety.
  • Collaboration: Highlight examples of your collaboration with teams and colleagues in solving complex engineering problems, emphasizing the collective application of knowledge and theory.
  • Mentoring and Training: If you've mentored or trained junior engineers, discuss how you've passed on your knowledge and helped them develop sound theoretical problem-solving skills.
  • Awards and Recognitions: If you've received awards or recognition for your contributions to solving engineering problems, mention them and explain the significance of these accolades.
